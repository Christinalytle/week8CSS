Top 5 Korean Dramas

According to Christina

Goblin: The Lonely and Great God

Synopsis

When Kim Shin is faced with death instead of honor after fighting a mighty battle, God turns Kim Shin into a Goblin instead, tasked with protecting certain people from al ife of misery. But immortality is wearing on Kim Shin, and he longs to find the prophesied human bride who can set his soul free.

Review

This is by far the best K-Drama out there. It's fulll of magic, romance, and comedy. Gong Yoo who plays Goblin is the best part of this show.

Healer

Synopsis

A "night courier" with the codename "Healer" who possesses top-notch fighting skills, a reporter from a second-rate tabloid news website, and a famous journalist at a major broadcast station try to uncover the truth from a 1992 incident and a series of present day murders, they grow into honest reporters who try to blur the lines of conflict between truth and reality, even if that means fighting media honchos.

Review

This K-Drama is for everyone. It has action, mystery, and romance. Ji Chang-Wook steals the show and is fantastic. It's a drama that you will want to binge watch all weekend.

Coffee Prince

Synopsis

Eun Chan is a licensed taekwondo instructor who parts time as a delivery woman. One day, she comes across a delivery order that leads her to Han Gyeol, an heir to a conglomerate. Her boyish appearance deceives everyone into thinking that she's a strong man with a petite figure. Han Gyeol, on the other hand, being relatively close to men leads his grandmother to think he's gay. Eun Chan and Han Gyeol's entanglement lead to them working in a cafe, which soon brews a different kind of love story. They face multiple problems along the way that bring themselves closer and their love stronger than ever.

Review

This is a very forward thinking K-Drama for its time. It's a wonderful story about love and loving a person, not a gender. Gong Yoo once again is memorizing, but the writing is what makes this a great show.

My Love from the Star

Synopsis

Do Min Joon is an alien who landed on Earth 400 years ago during the Joseon Dynasty period. He possesses a near perfect appearance and enhanced physical abilities involving his vision, hearing and speed. Later, he discovers that he has three months left on Earth. And that is when he meets Cheon Song Yi, the biggest Hallyu star in Korea.

Review

My Love from the Star is a really fun K-Drama. It has great comedy and romance (and some cool Korean history). Jun Ji Hyun is so funny and has a wonderful character arch.

After nine years of making Yeong Joon look good and stroking his very large ego, Min So decides to quit her job. Review

WWWSK is a good workplace K-Drama. It covers a lot of what a Korean workplace would be like. This is a fun, lighthearted comedy, with a great supporting cast. The dispute between the two brothers is a little problamatic and poor storytelling, but the relationship between Yeong Joon and Mi So is amazing.

K-Dramas for Software Developers/Tech Professionals

There are two great "tech" K-Dramas that are fun for computer software professionals. If you are looking for a great laugh and seeing women kill in in the industry, then I would check out Start Up and Search:WWW.
